BACHELOR OF SCIENCE IN SOCIAL WORK

A four-year degree program in the Philippines focuses on studying social units like families and communities, along with the various issues affecting them. It examines how social work can contribute to improving or resolving these conditions. Pursuing this degree will enable you to understand the structure of Philippine society, the historical context of poverty and underdevelopment, and the challenges faced by underrepresented groups such as people with disabilities, individuals from conflict-affected communities, members of the LGBTQ+ community, and indigenous peoples, alongside other marginalized populations.

PROGRAM EXPECTATIONS

Below are examples of courses that you may take in this program

  • Human Behavior and Social Environment
  • The Philippine Social Realities and Social Welfare
  • Filipino Personality and Social Work
  • Social Deviation and Social Work
  • Social Change and Development Perspectives
  • Social Welfare Policies, Programs, and Services
  • Social Welfare Project Program Development and Management
  • Social Work Statistics
  • Social Welfare Agency Administration
  • Social Work Community Education and Training
